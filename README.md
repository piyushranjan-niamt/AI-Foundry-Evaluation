# Can AI Help a Foundry Engineer?

*A Practical Evaluation of AI Assistants for Casting Defects and Shop-floor Problem Solving*

## Overview

This project explores whether AI assistants can genuinely help fresh foundry engineers understand practical engineering problems.

As a Metallurgy and Materials Engineering student, I often used AI to understand technical concepts during my studies. This made me curious about how reliable these responses actually were in real engineering situations. Instead of simply using AI, I decided to evaluate it using practical foundry questions that a Graduate Engineer Trainee (GET) might encounter.

## Objectives

- Evaluate AI responses for practical foundry engineering problems.
- Assess responses based on technical accuracy, clarity, completeness, and practical usefulness.
- Identify situations where AI can assist engineers and where engineering judgement is still essential.

## Topics Covered

- Casting Defects
- Melting Practice
- Heat Treatment
- Spectrometer Analysis
- Quality Inspection
- Shop-floor Troubleshooting

## Key Findings

- AI explains engineering concepts clearly and is useful for learning.
- AI performs well on common foundry problems but becomes less reliable for plant-specific situations.
- Engineering judgement remains essential for industrial decision-making.
- AI should be treated as a learning assistant rather than a replacement for experienced engineers.
